# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Community members who spot cats in their area — stray or otherwise — and want to help track and look out for them: tagging sightings with photo/GPS, flagging medical needs, voting to merge duplicate cat records. Used mobile-first, often outdoors, on the go.

## Product Purpose

Cat-A-Log is a crowdsourced registry for cats in the community. Logged-in users tag cats they spot with a photo and GPS location, view them on a map, and vote on whether two cat records are the same animal. Success looks like an accurate, up-to-date picture of the cats in an area — including welfare flags — built entirely from community contributions.

## Brand Personality

Warm, playful, communal — carried by the orange accent, Varela Round heading font, Duolingo/trading-card-style preview card, and bounce-in marker animations. Approachable and delightful, not clinical, but never at the expense of clarity around medical urgency.

## Anti-references

Sterile enterprise/admin-dashboard look; gamification that trivializes real animal-welfare signals; generic unstyled Material defaults.

## Design Principles

1. Community care over corporate polish — feels like a neighbor helping a neighbor.
2. Welfare signals are sacred — medical-urgency color coding never gets sacrificed for aesthetic consistency.
3. Mobile-first, thumb-first — built for one-handed outdoor use.
4. One consistent visual language — the glass/blur pill treatment recurs everywhere (nav, search, filter, preview card) so nothing feels bolted-on.
5. Motion with restraint — bounce/pop is the cat markers' "creature" personality; system chrome stays quieter so motion doesn't feel scattered.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

`prefers-reduced-motion` fallback on every animation (already implemented throughout), WCAG AA contrast on urgency colors, 44px minimum touch targets.
